Can I change the voice or lyrics in my song

Can I change the voice or lyrics in my song?

Edited

This isn't as easy as it sounds!

Suno currently outputs your song as one audio file containing all music and words (or just music, in the case of an instrumental). In this way, you can't really change the voice or lyrics in that version of your song.

One way to change up the voice, lyrics, or both, is to use the Reuse Prompt function. You can do this with any song in your library; just find the triple-dot icon next to the song you want to remake and choose Reuse Prompt.

This will automatically fill in all of the same details you had previously, but will also allow you to make changes before creating a new version. From here, you can edit Lyrics, Style of Music, and even the Title so you can easily identify each version you make with Reuse Prompt.

You can also change the ending of your song using the Extend function. This replaces the entire ending (music and lyrics) and allows you to decide where you want the original version to end and the new part begin. Check out this link for details!

Our team is exploring new options to edit your songs, including the ability to edit more than just the end and keep the song you like rather than getting something completely new. We can't wait to bring you more flexibility soon!

Can I replace a section of a song

Can I replace a section of a song?

Edited

Pro and Premier subscribers now have access to Replace Section, an editing feature that allows you to replace a part of your songs. While Crop is helpful to cut off the beginning or end of a song, Replace Section allows you to add or remove middle sections, change lyrics, etc. Here's an example of changing the lyrics:

From the Create or Library view, click the triple-dot More Actions menu and choose Edit and locate Replace Section

In the edit menu that appears, select Replace Section. As you click and drag to highlight the section you'd like to replace, the original lyrics are highlighted on the right, and added to the Lyrics box on the left.

You can modify the lyrics to the left, then click Recreate Section where you would normally find Create. The menu that appears next will confirm your changes.

After clicking Confirm, two new versions are created. Listen to both versions, then select the one you prefer!

After selecting you choice, Suno will create a new Whole Song including the updated section. These are tagged as Section and Full Song for easy identification.

Does Suno moderate songs

Does Suno moderate songs?

Edited

We support a welcoming community for anyone to make music, but inappropriate content may get flagged before you can generate a song.

At the moment, songs you generate are set to Link Only by default, and you can toggle them to Public at any time. By changing your songs to Public, they will appear in your public profile and can be featured in playlists on the Suno homepage. This is great to help you gain exposure and share your music with new audiences, but we still want to make sure the homepage is friendly for any listener.

While attempting to create music, your songs may not generate if they include:

  • The names of well known artists or people

  • Copyrighted or trademarked terms

  • Derogatory or defamatory terms

  • Excessive profanity

  • Other inappropriate topics or phrases

Some songs may not get flagged, but the community will alert us if posted material violates our Terms of Service or contains any of the points mentioned above. Moderation may include preventing a song from being generated, a team member asking for a song to be switched to Link Only, or the material being removed without warning. Similarly, Suno will follow DMCA guidelines for copyrighted material flagged by the original owner. For more details, please view our Terms of Service.

If you're unsure why certain content was flagged, feel free to reach our team at support@suno.com.

How do I crop a song

How do I crop a song?

Edited

Too much at the beginning or end of your song? Pro and Premier users can now try Crop to fix it! A new dedicated editing menu has been added to our desktop web version of Suno that allows you to crop a song in addition to changing the displayed lyrics for your song.

  1. Find a song in Library or in Create

  2. Click the triple-dot More Actions menu button

  3. Go to Edit and click Crop Song

  4. Click and drag across the waveform to make a selection. You can then click and drag from either end of the selection to adjust as needed.

  5. Click Crop Song, and new song will be added to your Library and Create views

Crop and the new Edit Details workflows are currently available while using Suno on a laptop or desktop. We are working to port these to tablet-web and mobile-web!

How do I make my song longer

How do I make my song longer?

Edited

Do you wish your songs had a different ending? You can make songs longer by using the Extend feature. Best of all, this can extend your maximum song length beyond 4 minutes, too!

Here's how:

From the Create or Library menu, locate the song you want to extend. Tap or click the triple-dot icon and find Extend in the menu that appears.

The next steps are written in the Lyrics field:

After entering your new lyrics, scroll down to add a title for your song extension. You can also choose specific point to extend from. When you're all set, tap Create.

After you've listened to the new parts, you tap the triple-dot icon next to your favorite extension and choose Get Whole Song to generate a full version with both parts together!

The finished product will have a Full Song tag added to it so you can easily identify the original, the extension (noted as Part 2), or the Full Song.

How do I share my music?

Edited

There are several ways to share your music with friends and new listeners alike!

By default, newly created songs are set to Private/Link Only. This means the songs are not publicly displayed on your profile, or featured on the Suno home page. You can still share a link to these songs, but another person can only see it if they have the direct link.

What is a Cover

What is a Cover?

Edited

Many popular songs are actually covers, but what does that mean?

A Cover is a new version of a song, made by someone other than the original artist. The original writer is always recognized as such, but there are occasions when a cover becomes more popular than the original! These tend to be made in an updated style, performed by a popular artist, or part of a viral trend.

In Suno, we've introduced Covers as a way for you to transform the style of an existing song while keeping the melody. This feature is in Beta, and currently only available for Pro and Premier users.

A few quick notes:

  • You can only use this feature for songs you created. It will not be available for you to use on another person's work.

  • This is a feature in progress, so we don't expect it to be perfect! It may take a few tries to get it just right.

  • Since we know you might have mixed results, the first batch of generations will be free (zero credits)!

How to use covers:

  • Visit your Library and find the song you'd like to cover

  • Click the triple-dot (More Actions) icon and hover over Create to find Cover Song

  • Add a new song style in Style of Music, update the title if needed, then click Create

More info at our blog here!

What is ReMi

What is ReMi?

Edited

ReMi (pronounced "ray me") is our latest lyric generating model, available in Beta via Suno's Custom mode on mobile/desktop web browsers.

What's with the name? If you've ever heard someone sing a musical scale using Do Re Mi Fa Sol La Ti Do, you've heard of solfÃ¨ge - a system for teaching pitch in western music. Though the origin of how we landed on ReMi remains lore, it makes perfect sense that our lyric model includes this nod to music in the name!

Is it actually different than before? Yes! You can still choose to generate lyrics with our classic model, but ReMi will give you very different creative results. You may find the lyrics a little edgier, too.

How do I access ReMi?

  • In the Create menu, ensure Custom mode is enabled.

  • In the Lyrics field, click Write with Suno. In the field that appears, write a description of the lyrics or topic you want to generate.

  • The dropdown menu on the right side will allow you to choose ReMi or the Classic model. Choose your lyrics model, then click Write Lyrics.

Remember: you don't have to keep generated lyrics exactly as they are. Try editing some lines, adding lyrics, or moving around certain phrases. You can change up as much as you want before making your song!
